The Australian Wave Atlas (AWAVEA) is built using AREMI.

AREMI is a website for map-based access to Australian spatial data relevant to the Renewable Energy industry - with a focus on Developers, Financiers, and Policy Makers. It is funded by the Australian Renewable Energy Agency and developed by NICTA (now CSIRO data61) in partnership with the Clean Energy Council with hosting being provided by Geoscience Australia. This website is an extension of the Australian Government's National Map initiative.

AREMI is a fully open architecture. When you access data through it, you are typically accessing the data directly from the government department or agency which is the custodian of that data.

AREMI...

  • provides easy access to authoritative and other spatial data to government, business and the public
  • facilitates the opening of data by federal, state and local government bodies
  • provides an open framework of geospatial data services that supports commercial and community innovation

How to use AWAVEA

Just go to http://awavea.csiro.au. The initial map view will show the study area (grey shaded) where wave data can be viewed and analyzed. Most operations are obvious, but you can click the Help button for specific instructions.

To see what data is available on AWAVEA, refer to the Data Tab in the AWAVEA itself. Click the Data tab and expand the Data Set group names. Wave specific data is available in Renewable Energy>Wave>Australian Wave Atlas.

To do analysis operations on wave data, refer to the Process Tab in the AWAVEA itself. Click the Processes tab and expand Web Processing Services from CSIRO O&A.

For further information email awavea.csiro.au. Feel free to send us feedback and bug reports.
